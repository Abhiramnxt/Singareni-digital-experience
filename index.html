When it started

Coal mining operations in the Singareni region began after reserves were discovered in 1871 by Dr. William King near the village of Yellendu. The Hyderabad (Deccan) Company Limited acquired the mining rights and initiated the first commercial mining on December 23, 1889. This date marks the start of the actual extraction work at the Singareni village site. The entire enterprise was then formalized when the company was incorporated as The Singareni Collieries Company Limited (SCCL) on December 23, 1920. This 1920 date is often cited as the official start of the modern company and is celebrated annually as Singareni Day. Today, SCCL is a significant government-owned entity, providing a critical source of energy to South India. The company continues to grow, playing a pivotal role in both the regional economy and the national power sector.

How Coal Is Extracted Underground

Underground coal mining involves extracting coal from deep beneath the Earth’s surface.
Miners construct shafts and tunnels to reach the coal seams.
Specialized machines are used to cut and remove the coal.
The extracted coal is transported to the surface using conveyor belts or mine carts.
Structural supports are installed to prevent the tunnel roof from collapsing.
Ventilation systems provide clean air and remove harmful gases.
Lighting and communication systems help maintain safe working conditions.
Workers are required to wear protective equipment at all times.
Regular inspections ensure the safety and stability of the mine.
This method allows access to coal that cannot be reached by surface mining.
Advanced monitoring technology helps detect movement in the rock layers.
Emergency procedures are in place to protect workers during hazards.
Underground mining continues to play an important role in supplying energy needs.

Kothagudem Area (Bhadradri Kothagudem District)

Introduction

The Singareni – Kothagudem District Area is one of the most significant coal mining regions in the state of Telangana, India, operated by the Singareni Collieries Company Limited (SCCL). Known for its rich reserves of high-quality non-coking coal, this region has played a crucial role in powering the industrial and energy sectors of the state and the nation. Over the decades, SCCL has transformed Kothagudem into a center of technological innovation, sustainable mining, and socio-economic development. The area not only contributes to India’s energy security but also stands as a model for responsible mining, community welfare, and environmental conservation.

Mining Operations and Industrial Infrastructure

The Kothagudem area hosts several underground and opencast mines, operated with modern machinery and technology. Key mining projects include the Kothagudem Opencast Projects (OC-I, OC-II) and underground mining units. SCCL employs advanced mining methods like Longwall Technology and Continuous Miner systems to improve productivity and safety. The company ensures regular maintenance, mechanization, and automation to enhance efficiency and worker protection. A comprehensive coal transportation system is established with railway sidings and coal handling plants. Infrastructure facilities such as power supply, workshops, and communication systems are well-developed within the area. These robust industrial operations make Kothagudem one of the leading coal-producing areas in Telangana and India.

Geographical and Geological Importance

The Kothagudem area lies in Bhadradri Kothagudem district of Telangana. It forms a part of the Gondwana coalfields, rich in non-coking coal reserves. The terrain supports both opencast and underground mining operations. The region’s coal is ideal for thermal power generation and industrial use. Its strategic location ensures easy transportation and connectivity.

Known for its vast coal reserves and large-scale opencast operations, Ramagundam serves as a backbone for the state’s power generation and industrial growth. The region combines rich natural resources with advanced mining technology, making it a cornerstone of SCCL’s contribution to India’s energy sector and Telangana’s economic progress.

Mining Operations and Projects

The In-pit Crushing and Conveyor technology for Over Burden removal and coal extraction was commissioned for the first time in India at the Ramagundam Open Cast Mine with German assistance.Major mining units include Ramagundam Opencast Projects I, II, and III, along with several underground mines. SCCL employs Draglines, Shovels, Dumpers, and Surface Miners for efficient and large-scale production. Advanced Longwall Mining and Continuous Miner Systems are used in underground operations. A well-established Coal Handling Plant (CHP) ensures quality control and smooth dispatch. The area’s coal supports nearby NTPC Ramagundam Power Plant, one of India’s largest thermal power stations. These mining projects highlight SCCL’s efficiency, safety, and innovation in the coal sector.

SCCL plans to further expand and modernize the Ramagundam Area with automation and digital tools.
